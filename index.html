Small, Energetic, and a Dreamer

Why I Dream

I spent my first year at Cornell University slowly building confidence to major in computer science, and a second year wrestling my insecurities in my classes and grades. Seeing only intelligent, ambitious people in the major, I thought I couldn’t compete as a girl who was still unsure of the future. During my first taste of industry the summer after sophomore year, I realized there was so much more to computer science than what my classes taught. I was inspired and supported by people to dream more about the things that I could do. Now, in my third year at Cornell, I am starting to explore and dream more of what I can do with computer science. My greatest hope is that regardless of where life takes me, I will be confident enough in my field to love what I do and be able to make a difference in people.

PAST XP

PayPal SWE Internship

Summer 2019

This was my first internship! I was on the Guest-Checkout team, and I mainly did frontend web development work using JavaScript with React, Redux, and Emotion. I also expanded on the GraphQL server to support my features. My main project was expanding the new Checkout Lite app to additional markets by building the Know Your Customer fields. On top of that, I built out billing and shipping country selection support for Inline Guest Checkout. I learned how a team works in real life: how much energy is put into writing readable and long-lasting code, how any doubt or questions are immediately approached to the appropriate people, and how everyone is responsible for checking and double-checking each other’s work.

THINGS I'M PROUD OF

Top 10 Project in Embedded Systems

May 2019

The final project for my Embedded Systems class was open ended and only required that we wrote in C and that it reflected some of the class material. My partner Jonathan Moon and I implemented an air guitar that generates PWM sound waves to pitches according to buttons pressed and if strum is sensed. It was really tough getting started since we both hardly had any hardware experience. Starting the project the earliest, our first question was asking how to use resistors on the breadboard. From there, we had fun getting closer to our teaching assistants, tinkering with our hardware components, and practicing Nintendo songs on our finished product for the final presentation video.

2nd place in Call For Code Hackathon

Sept 2018

Call For Code is a hackathon for natural disaster prepardness. My team and I designed a centralized communication tool for victims and rescuers in the event of a natural disaster that doubles as a weather app for Kenya users. Though I was the only CS major on my team, we were fueled by a teammate’s passion for his country.
