ABOUT ME

I am a junior at Cornell University studying Computer Science in the College of Engineering. I only just entered the field, but I am excited to begin my journey as a woman in computer science! Right now, I do not have a clear picture of what I want to pursue after graduation. Computer Science is such a broad term since there are so many fields closely tied to Computer Science. However, I aspire to be like the passionate people in my family, especially my sister-in-law Cassidy Williams. My greatest hope is that regardless of my passion, I will be confident enough in my field to love what I do and be able to make a difference in people. As of now, I am interested in exploring UI and app development. I am still very open to learning other ways to apply computer science, especially in other fields such as medicine and education. I am goofy and naive, and I love sharing jokes, funny stories, or gems of obscure facts. I may be small, but my personality is full of energy. On Friday nights, I will be lip-syncing Disney and Weezer songs, playing board games, or rock climbing with friends.

THINGS I'M PROUD OF

Top 10 Project in Embedded Systems

May 2019

The final project for my Embedded Systems class was open ended and only required that we wrote in C and that it reflected some of the class material. My partner Jonathan Moon and I implemented an air guitar that generates PWM sound waves to pitches according to buttons pressed and if strum is sensed. I was really tough getting started since we both hardly had any hardware experience. We started the project the earliest, and our first question was asking how to use resistors on the breadboard.

2nd place in Call For Code Hackathon

Sept 2018

Call For Code is a hackathon for natural disaster prepardness. My team and I designed a centralized communication tool for victims and rescuers in the event of a natural disaster that doubles as a weather app for Kenya users.
